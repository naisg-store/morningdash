Los Angeles Morning Weather Brief

Los Angeles mornings are defined by the iconic Pacific marine layer — a low blanket of clouds and coastal fog that often burns off by late morning. This creates cool, damp starts especially in coastal neighborhoods and along the 405 and 101 corridors. Inland areas like the San Fernando Valley warm up faster. Commuters on the I-10, 405, 101, and 5 should monitor visibility near the coast and watch for gusty Santa Ana winds in the fall and winter.

Typical morning temperatures range from the mid-50s°F near the ocean to the low-to-mid 60s°F inland. Humidity is usually moderate, but drizzle or light rain is common in winter months.
